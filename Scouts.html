About Our Group

Our scout group is dedicated to providing young people with a range of challenging and fun activities. We believe in building confidence, fostering teamwork, and teaching valuable life skills in a supportive and inclusive environment.

From hiking and camping to community service projects and creative workshops, our program is designed to help every scout grow into a resilient, compassionate, and responsible individual. We welcome all young adventurers to join us!

Squirrels (4-6 years)

We are excited about the new Squirrels section for 4- and 5-year-olds. It's the first step on the scouting journey, offering fun, friendship, and skills for life through play and adventure.

Currently, we are looking for dedicated volunteers to help us launch this new section! Without a leader, we cannot open the Squirrels drey. If you are passionate about helping young people and would like to get involved, please reach out.

Beavers (6-8 years)

Beaver Scouts are young people aged between 6 and 8 years old. They meet weekly to take part in a wide range of activities, including games, crafts, and outdoor adventures.

Our Beaver Colony is the first step on the Scouting journey. It's all about making new friends, having fun, and learning new things. We focus on building confidence and exploring the world around us in a safe and friendly environment.

Popular activities include nature walks, building small projects, and earning badges. The program is designed to be gentle and engaging for this age group, encouraging them to 'fun and friends'.

Cubs (8-10.5 years)

Cub Scouts are young people aged between 8 and 10.5 years. They meet each week to enjoy a huge variety of activities, from camping and hiking to team games and creative challenges.

The Cub section builds on the foundations of Beavers, introducing more complex skills and bigger adventures. We aim to help Cubs develop teamwork, leadership, and problem-solving abilities.

Highlights of the Cub program include overnight camps, outdoor cooking, and earning challenge badges that test their skills in areas like navigation, first aid, and art. The motto for Cubs is 'do your best'.

Scouts (10.5-14 years)

Scouts are young people aged between 10.5 and 14 years. The Scout Troop is where young people get to take part in adventurous activities and develop skills for life.

The program is more independent and scout-led, with a focus on personal growth and leadership. We take part in a wide range of activities, including expeditions, water sports, and climbing.

Scouts are encouraged to plan their own activities and work together in patrols. This section is all about pushing personal boundaries, gaining confidence, and working towards the Chief Scout's Gold Award. The motto for Scouts is 'be prepared'.

Young Leaders (14-18 years)

Young Leaders are Explorer Scouts who volunteer to work with one of the younger sections, such as Beavers or Cubs. It's a fantastic opportunity to gain leadership skills, build confidence, and make a real difference.

The Young Leader scheme provides training and support, helping young people to learn how to run activities, manage a group, and act as role models for younger scouts. It's a great stepping stone into adult leadership.

Being a Young Leader is also a great way to complete the service section of the Duke of Edinburgh's Award and other awards. It's a valuable and rewarding experience for anyone interested in developing their skills and helping others.
